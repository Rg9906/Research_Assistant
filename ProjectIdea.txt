# Project Research Document

# Project Name

**PaperPilot AI**
*An Autonomous Multi-Agent Research Assistant for Discovering, Understanding and Learning Research Papers.*

---

# Vision

PaperPilot AI is an autonomous multi-agent research assistant that helps users learn any technical topic by discovering the most relevant research papers, understanding their contents, connecting ideas across multiple papers, and answering questions grounded entirely in the retrieved literature.

Unlike a traditional "Chat with PDF" application, PaperPilot AI is designed to become an intelligent research companion capable of acting like a graduate research assistant.

Instead of simply retrieving chunks from PDFs, the system will:

* Search academic literature.
* Rank papers by relevance.
* Download papers automatically.
* Read and understand papers.
* Build retrieval indexes.
* Summarize papers at multiple levels.
* Explain difficult concepts.
* Compare multiple papers.
* Generate learning roadmaps.
* Remember previous research sessions.
* Plan future learning paths.

The long-term objective is to understand and implement modern Agentic AI systems while simultaneously learning Retrieval-Augmented Generation (RAG), agent orchestration, memory systems, tool usage, prompt engineering, document understanding, and LLM workflows.

Build a production-quality AI research assistant completely from scratch while understanding every component used.

# Development Principles

1. Build incrementally.
2. Understand every dependency.
3. Prefer official documentation over tutorials.
4. Write modular, testable code.
5. Keep agents independent and composable.
6. Validate outputs whenever possible.
7. Design for extensibility.
8. Document architectural decisions.
9. Avoid "magic" abstractions without understanding them.
10. Optimize for learning first and performance second.

---

# Final Objective

By the completion of PaperPilot AI, the project should evolve from a simple research-paper search tool into a full-fledged autonomous research companion capable of discovering knowledge, understanding scientific literature, teaching complex concepts, comparing research, maintaining long-term context, and guiding users through an entire learning journey.

More importantly, every subsystem should serve as a practical exploration of modern AI engineering, ensuring that the project becomes both a powerful research tool and a comprehensive educational platform for mastering contemporary LLM systems.
